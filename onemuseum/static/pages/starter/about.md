# About the OneMuseum

## History

The OneMuseum was conceptualised by Dr Roger Layton, CEO of Roger Layton Associates (RLA), in late 2018 in a paper at the South African Museums Association Conference held in Grahamstown, Eastern Cape Province.

This was long before the COVID-19 pandemic hit the planet, and it was an attempt to create a unified experience of access to museum knowledge and information by all potential users, and specifically to support the needs of education.

RLA conduct work in two primary sectors, museums and heritage, and basic education. The motivation for the OneMuseum was driven by the need for museums to increase their influence by moving into digital, and by the needs of schools, already becoming digital, to access trusted sources of information.

ETHER (eternal heritage) is our heritage business unit, which provided a wide range of services for the heritage sector, primarily in Southern Africa.

ETHER has been active in the field of digitization of museums and other heritage institutions for almost 15 years, which included the writing of the South African National Policy on the Digitisation of Heritage in 2011.

We are also active in museum professional development, conducted through a wide range of courses and workshop covering governance, collection management, and the user experience.

RLA was involved as an external party in a project for Europeana, the European Digital Library, and have learned much from this experience in the design in a digital repository which is more accessible to both contributors and to users.

## COVID-19

We find ourselves at this time in the midst of the worst pandemic in living memory, and this has necessitated a rapid shift towards digital technologies as the basis for life and work.

Most of us have been confined to homes for more than three months (100 days and counting as I write this in South Africa), and we have adapted our work, using Microsoft Teams and Zoom rather than travelling to meetings, and we have found that this is far more effective.

Almost all museums of the world have been shut down, and even when opening are restricted for safety and social distancing. It is highly likely that school visits will not be re-introduced for a long time, and this has led some museums to offer online lessons, and some are providing virtual reality guides.

The vast majority of museums are unprepared for the rapid transformation to digital and some have been considering how to move online by themselves rather than as a group.

We hope that the One-useum offers one alternative for rapidly going online, at almost no cost, which may become a stop-gap until the museum has developed its own capacity; or as a permanent online solution.

## Goals of the OneMuseum

The OneMuseum is a repository of a range of museum products, and is not limited to digital reproductions as is the case with Europeana. Rather, the focus is on preparing all forms of museum product to be quickly nprepared and uploaded, so that these can be available to the wider community of users.

### Short-Term Goal : Connecting Museums to Education

Our short-term goals it to enable the educational material provided by museums to schools and learners, almost excluvisely as part of school visits, to the wider range of educators and learners, including access across international borders.

The museum products for education currently include:

* Lessons and Lesson Plans
* Stories
* Biographies
* Online Events - which are included into the OneMuseum Calendar
* Experts - access to experts on various subjects

## Status of the OneMuseum

Following 18 months of development, we are now ready, in July 2020, to release the first version for usage, from which we plan to inprove and refine the service in many ways.

## Long-Term Sustainability of the OneMuseum

We have advised other institutions on the process of becoming a "Trusted Digital Repository" (TDR) and we now find ourselves in having to apply this knowledge to the OneMuseum.

Of particular concern at this time is that of governance continuity, and for this we will be establishing an initial Board of Trustees to assist us in the governance, management, strategic planning, risk management, deployment scaling, and other concerns which will come to the fore as the OneMuseum grows.

We are also limiting the initial growth, to avoid the issue of "speed-wobble", and will be making refinements in the technology model to ensure good performance and scalability.
