# OneMuseum for Schools

We are positioning OneMuseum initially as a resource for basic education - primary and secondary schools, on the K-12, R-12, or Years R-12 depending on the national curriculum standards

All schools are directed to deliver learners who meet curriculum requirements, using a variety of teaching and learning support approaches and materials which include:

* textbooks
* educator notes
* classroom activities
* online resources
* applications
* field trips
* visits by experts

We are implementing digital transformation in schools, with a focus on special needs education - including accessibility and inclusive classrooms, and we have noted the continued reliance on textbooks in this digital age, and the approach of teachers teaching learners who learn by copying and repeating back what has been learned.

Benejami Bloom published his Taxonomy of Learning Objectives in the 1950s, which as revised and updated in 2000. This provides a six-level model for cognitive abilities, which ranges from REMEMBER to CREATE.

The focus on modern education is shifting towards the upper-band of Bloom's model, in which creation of products by learners, perhaps using physical or digital materials, and perhaps using coding, is preferred over attention on the lower-band.

We follow the 21st century learning design framework (
[21CLD](https://educationblog.microsoft.com/en-us/tag/21cld/)
) which redefines education to complement the curriculum by 
highlight six skills which are becoming increasingly important in the 21st century.

The 21CLD does not impact WHAT is taught, but rather HOW it is taught and what should be the outcomes
beyond the curriculum minimum.

We are conducting a range of virtual workshops for museums to assist with the preparation of
materials which meet the modern requirements for educational materials and resources to meet the Bloom Taxonomy
and the 21CLD Framework.

For schools we are running workshops on digital transformation, which include the shift
towards digital learning, with a focus on the Lesson Plan as the instrument of change.

The OneMuseum is a resource for basic education in a number of ways:
* linkage to the curriculum - currently addressing the South African national 
[CAPS](https://www.education.gov.za/Curriculum/CurriculumAssessmentPolicyStatements(CAPS).aspx)
curriculum.
* curriculum personnel and heads of department to explore the potential content on the OneMuseum
* educators to utilize the content in their lesson plans and delivery
* learners to research materials on the OneMuseum as part of projects

It will take some time to prepare the content for the OneMuseum, and we will be starting small. We will be measuring progress on specific areas of the curriculum to look for gaps and to find sources which can fill these gaps.
