# Content Policy

The  is committed to provide high-quality, accurate, and trusted content from museums and other heritage institutions.

We plan to achieve this using the following measures:

* engaging with museums to assist them with content preparation and uploading
* defining minimum standards for content
* maintaining a common set of vocabularies to enhance the discoverability of the content
* supporting the evolution of these vocabularies as additional information is loaded
* maintaining the curriculum at the level of topics
* providing the ability to link content to topics
* providing training for museums on how to increase their digital content on the OneMuseum
* tracking of usage of the OneMuseum and reporting back to museums
* arranging for regular independent reviews of the OneMuseum
* continually expanding the range of services
* developing a third-party support community
* engaging with collection management system vendors to provide dynamic publishing of content to the OneMuseum
* ensuring a secure environment for uploading and ingestion of content with approval requirements
* providing for user content to be added connected to the museum-provided content, which is fed back to the museums to assist with improvement of their documentation
* educational materials should be designed for a specific audience with a clear purpose - such as lesson or project
* aiming to provide a complete support system for basic education

Content will be guided by the following principles:

* authentic history will be provided and placed into context
* contested history should be identified as such and not positioned as the only version of the past
