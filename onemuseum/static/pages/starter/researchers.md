# OneMuseum for Researchers

Researchers require more in-depth materials than schools. The OneMuseum is structured to provide curated content, prepared by museum education specialists, to schools, and raw content, including online collections, to the researchers.

The strategic plan for OneMuseum is firstly to address the needs of the very large number of school learners in South Africa, and potentially throughout the world, and then, as the museums mature in their online capability, to provide the details catalogues.

To develop these catalogues is a longer process, requiring that museums inventorize and catalogue their collections, together with a minimal level of digitization and reproduction, to the extent required by the research community.

We propose that every museum should develop its own full online content in its own repository, and also provide the information required for the OneMuseum. This will then provide for basic information to be discovered on the OneMuseum, with the researcher then pointed to the museums which can assist with more detailed content.

Our first initiative is to put the collections online as a single record for each collection in each museum, which wikl assist researchers by pointing them to the places to seek further information through other forms of contact, including email, remote meetings, and physical visits.
