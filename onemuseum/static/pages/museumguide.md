# Guide for Contributing Museums and Non-Museums

We welcome you to publish your content to the OneMuseum repository to provide this content to your
audience and to new national and a world-wide users who can benefit from access to
trusted information and knowledge which you keep in your collections.

It is time to unlock the full value of the collections, and to move away from the approach of selective curation.

We can support your digital transformation, including not only the publishing of your content
but also the internal digital transformation of your policies, procedures and data.

## Familiarise yourself with using OneMuseum

You should read the
[User Guide](userguide)
first, and also use the OneMuseum to become familiar with the operations and the
type of data which is stored and maintained.

## Deciding what you should be publishing to OneMuseum

This will help you to determine what you can add, and in what sequence, and where you will get the data from.

Our strategy is to add the content which can be used by educators and learners in primary and secondary schools as the initial priority. This will include lessons, stories, biographies, and museum events of an educational nature.

However, your strategic imperatives may be different, based on your audience, and you should prepare for this in your own way.

The **LESSONS** will be linked directly to curriculum elements, and **STORIES** and **BIOGRAPHIES** can be used for more in-depth work such as projects.

All of this content will be linked to one or more of the **FILTERS** which will be used for the users to find these quickly.

## Preparing content

Each type of content is created using a simple markup language we have developed called SDF - the Simple Data Format - which is designed to be written by humans (or applications) and read by applications. This makes the SDF language highly suited for rapid data capture, especially for those museums with no cataloguing systems in place. This is used in preference to other languages used for data transfer, such as CSV and Excel which lack a semantic basis, and XML and JSON, which are over-complicated for human capture.

If you have data systems in place, then we can assist you with the conversion of these data systems into the formats required by OneMuseum
