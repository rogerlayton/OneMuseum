# Guide for Users of the OneMuseum

## A Quick Walkthrough

You will create an account using your email and a password, and this will allow you access
to the OneMuseum.

Once you log in you are taken to the OneMuseum User Command Center, which is a single point
of access to all of the content and other services.

The content is divided into five categories:

* LEARN: learn about the OneMuseum
* DISCOVER: explore all of the content on the OneMuseum
* FILTER: select filters and find information which is connected to the filters, such as the Curriculum
* REFERENCE: access to reference sources, such as the Chenhall Nomenclature - more to be added over time

## What Information you can Find

* MUSEUMS: a list of museums, with relevant details where these are available - this list will be built up over time and museums provide their details.
* COLLECTIONS: a list of the collections in museums, connected to the filter values
* EXHIBITIONS: a list of the exhibitions in the museums, including permanent, temporary, and virtual
* ITEMS: a list of items which the museums have provided from their collections
* STORIES: content provided as short stories on the basis of the collections of the museum
* LESSONS: linked to the curriculum, using one or more approaches to educational materials - lesson plans with content, or projects to interface with the museum content
* PUBLICATIONS: formal publications of the museum, either as a list, or as full-text content
* EXPERTS: a list of the experts of the museum, and their subject matter skills
* MUSEUM EVENTS: part of the calendar of events for the museum community, including virtual events
* BIOGRAPHIES: of persons and organisations who are relevant to the museum

## Filters for Selecting Content

The classes of filters are a core element of the OneMuseum, providing a common approach for categorizing the content, so that these can be used as filters during the search for content.

These are developed and built up as the need arises.

* NAMED PLACES: a vocabulary of place names, for all types of places
* NAMED EVENTS: names which we give to events, such as wars, geological periods, archaeological perios, historical events and periods, etc.
* PERSONS/ORGANISATIONS: a list of persons who are referenced in the content and organisations
* SUBJECTS: a general subject vocabulary for items now found in the other classes
* ITEM TYPES: the types of items, such as within a homogeneous collection
* CURRICULUM ELEMENTS: topics from the various topics in the curriculum

## Selecting Favourites

When you search for information in one of the types of content, or using the filters, you can select the individual content as favourites.

You need to open up the details of the content and then clicking on the star will make this a favourite or remove this as a favourite.

Your favourites are available with the MANAGE menu.
